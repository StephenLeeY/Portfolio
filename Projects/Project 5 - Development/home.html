Usability Principles

The 3 main usability principles I learned in this class was Learnabililty, Usability, and Memorability.

First, I tackled learnability by creating a clear visual hierarchy. Utilizing a color theme and the knowledge that users typically navigate top to bottom and left to right was key in knowing where to put my filter and sorting options. I made sure the navbar was in a different color scheme to stand out and had clear rectangular containers for filters ordered in importance from top to bottom.

Second, I tackled usability by making sure all the interactive components are clearly marked as interactible and all the components have distinctive colors from their background. One example of this was to add + and - buttons to the top right of item cards, even when the entire card was clickable. Another example was changing the card's color to dark pink to avoid it blending in with the light pink background.

Finally, I tackled memorability by keeping things as simple and consistent as possible. All the tabs functioned the same way and adding or removing cards was as simple as clicking the cards. It was clear how things functioned as the application was immediately responsive in showing the cart at all times.
